CARBOHYDRATES: BASIC STRUCTURE

Carbohydrates are made of building blocks called monosaccharides, similar to Minecraft blocks!

Elements Involved: Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), and Oxygen (O)

Formula: CnH2nOn (in simple sugars)

Carbohydrates can be simple (like sugar) or complex (like starch), just like how Minecraft has simple blocks and complex structures!

CARBOHYDRATES: MAIN FUNCTIONS

  • Primary energy source for the body (like coal and redstone in Minecraft)
  • Store energy for later use
  • Provide structure in plants (like cellulose)
  • Help with communication between cells

Just as coal powers your Minecraft furnace, glucose powers your body's cells!

CARBOHYDRATES: INTERESTING FACTS

Carbohydrates make up about 80% of a plant's dry weight!

Your brain needs about 130 grams of carbohydrates each day to function properly.

Fiber is a type of carbohydrate your body can't digest, but it helps your digestive system work properly!

Why They're Important

Carbohydrates are essential for life because they provide the quick energy needed for all cellular activities. Without them, your body would be like a Minecraft character with an empty hunger bar!

PROTEINS: MAIN FUNCTIONS

  • Build and repair body tissues (like repairing your tools in Minecraft)
  • Form enzymes that speed up chemical reactions
  • Transport molecules throughout the body
  • Help with immune function (like fighting off creepers!)

Hemoglobin, a protein in your blood, carries oxygen throughout your body.

Without proteins, your body couldn't build or repair itself. They're like the crafting table of your body, essential for creating and fixing everything!

LIPIDS: BASIC STRUCTURE

Lipids are a diverse group including fats, oils, and waxes that don't dissolve in water, like how oil and water separate in Minecraft!

C
H
O

Elements Involved: Mostly Carbon (C) and Hydrogen (H), with some Oxygen (O)

Most lipids have many carbon-hydrogen bonds, which store lots of energy, like how TNT stores explosive power in Minecraft!

LIPIDS: MAIN FUNCTIONS

  • Store energy long-term (like a chest full of valuable items)
  • Insulate the body and maintain temperature (like wool blocks)
  • Form cell membranes (like the walls of your Minecraft house)
  • Help absorb certain vitamins (A, D, E, K)
  • Produce hormones that send signals in the body

Lipids store more than twice the energy of carbohydrates per gram!

LIPIDS: FOOD SOURCES

You can find lipids in these Minecraft-like food sources:

  • Oils (like from seeds)
  • Butter (from milk, which exists in Minecraft)
  • Fatty meats ü•© (like Minecraft porkchops)
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Avocados

LIPIDS: INTERESTING FACTS

Your brain is about 60% fat, showing how important lipids are for nervous system function!

Lipids help form the myelin sheath that protects nerve cells, like armor protects you in Minecraft!

Cholesterol, a type of lipid, is essential for making vitamin D and hormones.

Why They're Important

Lipids are vital for energy storage, cell structure, and protection. They're like the diamond armor of biomolecules - providing protection and durability to your body!

NUCLEIC ACIDS: BASIC STRUCTURE

Nucleic acids (DNA and RNA) contain the genetic instructions for life, like the command blocks in Minecraft!

C
H
O
N
P

Elements Involved: Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), Oxygen (O), Nitrogen (N), and Phosphorus (P)

Nucleic acids are made of nucleotides, which contain a sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base (A, T, G, C in DNA).

NUCLEIC ACIDS: MAIN FUNCTIONS

  • Store genetic information (like a redstone memory circuit)
  • Pass genetic traits to offspring
  • Direct protein synthesis
  • Control all cellular activities

DNA is like the master blueprint for all the builds in your body!

NUCLEIC ACIDS: WHERE TO FIND THEM

Unlike other biomolecules, we don't typically eat nucleic acids for nutrition. They are found:

  • In every cell of your body
  • In cells of all living things
  • In some viruses

When we eat foods with cells (virtually everything!), we consume some nucleic acids, but they're broken down during digestion.

NUCLEIC ACIDS: INTERESTING FACTS

If you stretched out all the DNA in your body, it would reach to the moon and back about 8,000 times!

DNA has a double helix structure, like a twisted ladder!

Your DNA sequence is 99.9% identical to every other human but that 0.1% difference makes you unique!

Why They're Important

Nucleic acids are essential because they contain the instructions for building and operating every living thing. They're like the command blocks and redstone circuits of life!
